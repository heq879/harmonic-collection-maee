Dark rain,

Not so much rain,

But rather a cascade of tresses falling from the bathtub—

Lustrous, voluminous strands of sabled skin,

A fragrance of how it was peeled away from the skull.


Red moon,

Not an ink-stained hue,

But rather a scar stitched on the knee,

A droplet of blood through its veins,

A congested scab that has not yet been removed.


Green dress,

Singing of the nightingale,

Melody unfolds like the spread of snowed fur under her hill—

Softness of the kitty belch as she feels herself sink,

The beginning of a forborne carnage.


Pale belly,

Frostbite, almost transparent—

Clearer than the unrinsed foam in between palms.

Gazed, lifted up, settled back down,

It was never put so lightly, yet opened so sharply.


Crystalline nail,

Extended into the thickened foam,

Waited for the fur to unfurl,

Stretching from the table to beyond the door,

Carpet of a velvety membrane.


Is it carnage,

By screwing its immature limbs,

Puncturing its aching belly,

Wiping its decaying flesh,

The tiny, unfilled kitten,


Isn’t it carnage?

Organs of life laid bare,

Sticked alongside the droplets of the moon.

A silent fall, one, two, three…

Smear of dark rain.
